Replace this paragraph with your abstract — UofT's School of Graduate
Studies caps the abstract at 350 words for both M.Sc. and Ph.D.
theses. The abstract is a single page on which you summarise the
problem, the contribution, and the result. ProQuest indexes it
verbatim for everyone who searches your work, so write it for someone
who hasn't read your introduction.

A working draft template: state what is known, what is missing, what
you did, what you found, and what it means. A reader in a related
field should recognise the problem from the first sentence and the
contribution from the last.
